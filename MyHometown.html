Little Hong Kong of Borneo

Sandakan is often referred to as "Little Hong Kong" due to its historical significance as a bustling port town during the British colonial era.
The town's architecture and vibrant street life were reminiscent of Hong Kong, attracting traders and settlers from various parts of Asia.

Bornean Sun Bear Conservation Centre

Located in Sepilok, Sandakan, BSBCC rescues
and rehabilitates sun bears that
were kept illegally as pets or captive animals.
The centre helps them relearn natural behaviours while educating visitors about conservation.
Surrounded by Sandakan’s rich forests
and wildlife, it highlights the town’s

Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre

Located in Sandakan, Sabah, is one of Southeast Asia’s most important sanctuaries for orphaned and rescued orangutans.
The centre focuses on rehabilitation, teaching young orangutans vital survival skills before releasing them back into the wild.
Surrounded by lush rainforest, Sepilok offers visitors a rare chance to observe these gentle primates up close while supporting vital wildlife conservation.

Agnes Keith House

The Agnes Keith House in Sandakan is a beautifully preserved historic colonial-style home that once belonged to the renowned American author Agnes Newton Keith. Today, it serves as a museum dedicated to showcasing her life, literary works, and the rich history of Sabah during the British North Borneo era. Visitors can explore the rooms and furnishings that reflect the lifestyle of the early 20th century, offering a unique glimpse into colonial life in Sandakan. Surrounded by lush greenery and well-maintained gardens, the house provides a tranquil and reflective atmosphere, allowing guests to immerse themselves in the stories of the past and appreciate the cultural heritage of the region. It stands not only as a tribute to Agnes Keith’s contributions to literature but also as a symbol of Sandakan’s historical and cultural legacy.

Turtle Islands Park

Turtle Islands Park, located on Selingan Island off the coast of Sabah, Malaysia, is a protected area famous for its green and hawksbill turtles. Libaran Island is also part of the park. These islands serve as safe nesting grounds for endangered turtles and offer visitors a chance to learn about turtle conservation. Tourists can see turtles lay their eggs at night, witnessing this amazing natural event up close. With pristine beaches, clear waters, and rich marine life, the park is both a natural wonder and an educational destination.
